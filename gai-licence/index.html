The GAI Licence

The Generative Artificial Intelligence Licence — the MIT Licence with two additions, in the spirit of factor VI.

Generative AI is trained on the combined knowledge of all of humanity, so what it derives from that inheritance must be returned to humanity. The GAI Licence is the MIT Licence with two additions: any derivative you distribute stays open, bound by this same licence, and AI-written material keeps its authorship marker (🤖, or its plain-text form [AI]).

It is not MIT — don't describe software under it as "MIT-licensed" — and it has not been through OSI review. Its share-alike follows the work, not your tools, and only when you distribute: running it, changing it, keeping it to yourself owes nothing.

This page is the licence's stable home: the Eleven Messenger project stewards it and publishes each version here, so downstream users can always verify which text governs a copy they hold.

A NOTE, PLAINLY (not part of the terms)

Condition 2 makes this a different licence from MIT — do not describe
software under it as "MIT-licensed". It has not been through OSI review,
and it is not compatible with the GPL family in either direction: its
conditions are "further restrictions" there, and the GPL's are here. The
share-alike follows the work, not your tools: whether you extend the
Software by hand or with Generative AI, a Derivative Work you give to
others stays open under this licence, because this software's own lines were
distilled from humanity's commons and no one downstream may fence off that
inheritance. Nothing here restricts what you do in private — use it, change
it, keep it to yourself; the obligation begins only when you Distribute.
That trigger is deliberate: running the Software, or a Derivative Work, as
a service for others is use, not Distribution, and owes nothing back — this
licence polices passing the work on, not running it. And yes — this licence
was itself written by a generative artificial intelligence, on the ideas,
instruction, and editing of humans, which is why it opens with a 🤖.

The Marker Specification

Condition 3 binds a versioned convention for AI-authorship markers. The current specification is version 1.0: a visible 🤖 (or [AI] where the emoji can't survive) marks AI-written material, a marker on a heading cascades over what's beneath it, absence of a marker claims nothing, and a marker comes off only when a human has substantially rewritten what it marked.
